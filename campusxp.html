Project Background

In 2018, I transformed my campus blog into a magazine called Campus Experience Magazine (CampusXP). The aim was to highlight the unique experiences of Nigerian students and provide valuable tips for navigating campus life. However, the cost of hiring a designer was prohibitive, which prompted me to teach myself design skills to create the magazine independently.

Project Brief

My team and I needed a fresh and engaging design that resonated with the student audience. The magazine aimed to combine informative articles with a visually appealing layout, making it easy to read and navigate. I set out to create a brand identity that captured the essence of campus life while remaining accessible to students.

Goals
  • Develop a cohesive magazine layout that reflects the vibrant life of Nigerian students.
  • Ensure the design is engaging and easy to navigate, enhancing the reader's experience.
  • Create a brand identity that can grow with the magazine as it evolves.
Process
  • Self-Education: I dedicated time to learn graphic design principles through online courses and tutorials, focusing on magazine layouts and typography.
  • Concept Development: I brainstormed design ideas and created mood boards to visualize the magazine's aesthetic, emphasizing youthfulness and vibrancy.
  • Design Iteration: I designed the first issue, seeking feedback from peers. This iterative process refined the layout and visual elements.
  • Production: Over a year and six months, I produced the second to fourth issue, incorporating lessons learned along the way and ensuring each edition improved upon the last.
Unique Solution

The final design of CampusXP featured a bold color palette and dynamic layouts, incorporating student photography and relatable content. The magazine's branding reflected the energetic spirit of campus life, making it not only informative but also visually appealing. This approach helped foster a sense of community among readers.

Tools Used
  • Adobe InDesign: For layout design and magazine production
  • Adobe Photoshop: For image editing and enhancements.
  • Canva: For creating promotional materials and social media graphics.
Experience Gained

This journey not only allowed me to develop valuable design skills but also fostered a sense of connection among students. I am proud to have contributed to the campus experience and provided a platform for student voices.
